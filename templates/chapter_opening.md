# Template: Novel Chapter Opening Format

## Structure Options

### Option A: Character-Focused Opening

```markdown
[Character name] was [doing mundane thing].

[Brief expansion of mundane thing with specific detail]

[Sentence revealing something will interrupt this]

[The interruption, stated flatly]

[Character's reaction, which fails to grasp significance]
```

### Option B: Cosmic Scale Opening

```markdown
[Universal or galactic scale statement]

[Zoom to slightly smaller scale]

[Zoom to planet/region scale]

[Zoom to specific location]

[Introduce character doing something trivial in this cosmic context]
```

### Option C: Philosophical Opening

```markdown
[Profound question]

[Apparent answer or conventional wisdom]

[Subversion of that answer]

[Introduction of character who embodies the subversion]

[Character doing something that demonstrates the point]
```

---

## Option A Example: Character-Focused

Arthur Dent was having a bath.

This was unusual. Not the bath itself—Arthur was quite fond of baths, finding in them a combination of warmth and privacy that the rest of the universe seemed determined to deny him—but the timing. It was 3:47 in the afternoon, which is not a normal bath time by any reasonable standard.

He had decided, approximately seventeen minutes earlier, that nothing particularly important was going to happen for the rest of the day.

The doorbell rang.

Arthur considered ignoring it. The water was precisely the right temperature—not too hot, not too cold, but that perfect lukewarm that suggested the universe, for once, was on his side. The doorbell rang again. Arthur sighed, which is the internationally recognised sound of accepting defeat.

## Option B Example: Cosmic Scale

In the unfashionable end of the western spiral arm of the galaxy, tucked between a dying red dwarf and a planet that had once been quite popular before its sun exploded, there floated a small piece of rock.

The rock was unremarkable. It had been unremarkable for approximately four billion years, which is a long time to be unremarkable by anyone's standards.

On this rock, there was a continent. On this continent, there was a country. In this country, there was a city. In this city, there was a street. On this street, there was a house. In this house, there was a man.

The man was eating toast.

He had no idea, as he spread marmalade with the careful attention of someone who has nothing better to do, that the unremarkable piece of rock he lived on was about to become very remarkable indeed.

## Option C Example: Philosophical

The universe, it is generally agreed, is quite large.

This is not particularly useful information. Knowing that something is large does not tell you what to do about it, any more than knowing that your problems are small—cosmically speaking—makes them feel any less like problems.

Gerald Pemberton understood this better than most. Gerald was a philosopher, or had been, until he discovered that thinking about the meaning of existence paid significantly less than not thinking about it.

He was currently working as an accountant.

The numbers, at least, stayed where you put them. This was more than could be said for meanings, which had a tendency to wander off precisely when you needed them most.

## Key Elements

1. **Sentence variation:** Mix short punches with longer builds
2. **Specific details:** Bath temperature, marmalade spreading, 3:47 PM
3. **Foreshadowing deflation:** Hint at what's coming while character remains oblivious
4. **Scale contrast:** Cosmic importance vs. mundane concern
5. **Character voice:** Through their concerns, reveal their nature
6. **Reader positioning:** We know something they don't

## Vocabulary Targets

- Include at least one of: quite, rather, rather, approximately, precisely
- Avoid: very, really, suddenly, amazing, incredible
- Use British spellings and vocabulary
