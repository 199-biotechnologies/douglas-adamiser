# Template: Cosmic Zoom Structure

## The Technique

The cosmic zoom is Adams' signature opening device: begin at the largest possible scale (universe, galaxy, existence itself), then progressively narrow focus until arriving at something absurdly specific and mundane.

The humor comes from the contrast between cosmic insignificance and human concern.

---

## Structure

```markdown
[COSMIC LEVEL: Universal/galactic statement]
↓
[STELLAR LEVEL: Solar system or regional space]
↓
[PLANETARY LEVEL: Earth or equivalent]
↓
[REGIONAL LEVEL: Continent/country/city]
↓
[LOCAL LEVEL: Specific location]
↓
[INDIVIDUAL LEVEL: One person]
↓
[TRIVIAL DETAIL: Their mundane concern]
```

---

## Formula

```markdown
[Scale 1] contains [impressive number] of [Scale 2].

[Scale 2], in turn, [diminishing importance statement].

[Scale 3] is [unflattering description], notable only for [something unimpressive].

On [Scale 4], there exists [dismissive description of location].

In [specific place], [character name] was [doing trivial thing].

[Trivial thing] was, in fact, [elaborate description that makes it seem important].

It wasn't.
```

## Worked Example

The universe contains approximately four hundred billion galaxies, give or take. Nobody has counted them recently, and frankly, after the first few trillion stars, most sentient beings tend to lose interest.

The Milky Way is one of these galaxies. It is, by galactic standards, thoroughly unremarkable—a spiral of moderate size, middling age, and no particular ambition. It has never won any awards.

Orbiting an average yellow star in an unfashionable arm of this unremarkable galaxy, there is a small, damp planet called Earth. Its inhabitants have, at various points, believed themselves to be the centre of the universe. This is the sort of belief that the universe finds rather endearing, in the way that adults find it endearing when children believe they can fly.

On this planet, there is a country called England. England is notable primarily for its weather, which manages to be disappointing in every season, and its people, who have turned complaining about the weather into a form of communication.

In a town in the south of England, in a house that was neither particularly old nor particularly new, in a kitchen that smelled faintly of toast, a man named Harold Finch was attempting to open a jar of marmalade.

The lid was stuck.

This was, Harold felt, typical. Not just typical of this particular jar, or this particular morning, but typical of a universe that had, across four hundred billion galaxies and countless trillions of years, somehow conspired to produce a jar lid that wouldn't budge.

The universe, naturally, was indifferent. It had other things on its mind.

Harold twisted harder.

## Variations

### The Reverse Zoom (Mundane → Cosmic)

Start with trivial detail, progressively reveal its cosmic significance.

```
Harold opened the jar.

This was, in itself, unremarkable. People open jars every day.

What made it remarkable was that the jar, as it happened, was not from Earth.

In fact, the jar was not from this galaxy.

The marmalade inside was made from a fruit that grew on a planet that no longer existed, in a solar system that had collapsed into a black hole three billion years ago, by a species that had gone extinct before Earth had formed.

Harold spread it on his toast.

It tasted quite good.
```

### The Interrupted Zoom

Start the cosmic zoom but interrupt it before reaching the individual.

```
The universe is vast. The galaxy is large. The solar system is—

Actually, none of that matters at the moment.

What matters is that someone, somewhere, has just done something extremely stupid.
```

## Key Principles

1. **Each scale should diminish the previous:** Don't just list scales—make each one less impressive
2. **Unflattering specificity:** The more specific, the more deflating
3. **Numbers should be overwhelming:** "Four hundred billion" not "many"
4. **Final focus should be utterly trivial:** Jam jars, sock drawers, queue positions
5. **Tone remains deadpan throughout:** The cosmic and the trivial receive equal narrative weight
6. **The universe doesn't care:** End by confirming cosmic indifference

## Word Count Guide

- Cosmic → Individual zoom: 200-400 words
- Each scale level: 1-3 sentences
- Final trivial focus: Can expand into scene or remain brief
