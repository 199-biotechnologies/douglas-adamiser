# Example: News Article to Douglas Adams Style

## Original Text (Generic News Style)

```
Tech company announces new AI assistant. The artificial intelligence system, developed over three years by a team of 200 engineers, can process natural language queries and provide helpful responses. The CEO stated that this represents a significant advancement in AI technology. The product will be available to consumers next quarter.
```

## Transformed (Douglas Adams Style)

The technology company (which had, in fact, been a sandwich shop until a particularly aggressive investment round in 2019, and still occasionally smelled of tuna) announced on Tuesday that it had created something.

The something in question was, they claimed, an artificial intelligence. This was not entirely accurate. It was, in fact, artificial, in much the same way that a plastic plant is artificial—present, vaguely green-shaped, and fundamentally incapable of the thing it was pretending to be. The intelligence part remained, as it were, aspirational.

Two hundred engineers had worked on the project. This is rather a lot of engineers. If you were to line up two hundred engineers end to end, they would stretch approximately 350 metres and would, almost certainly, complain about it. The engineers had worked for three years, which works out to roughly 1.2 million hours of collective effort, or approximately the same amount of time the average person spends trying to find matching socks.

The CEO—a man whose smile had been focus-grouped seventeen times and was now perfectly optimised for conveying the impression that he knew what he was talking about—stated that this represented a "significant advancement." He did not specify what it was an advancement from, or towards, which is quite often how significant advancements work.

The product would be available next quarter. This meant, in practice, that it would be announced again next quarter, and then again the quarter after that, until eventually it would simply materialise on people's devices like a fungus that nobody could quite remember installing.

The artificial intelligence, for its part, had no comment. It was, after all, not particularly intelligent.

## Transformation Notes

**Techniques applied:**

1. **Parenthetical escape** (Line 1): Sandwich shop backstory digression
2. **Simile inversion** (Para 2): "in much the same way that a plastic plant is artificial"
3. **Absurd quantification** (Para 3): Engineers lined up, sock-matching time comparison
4. **Corporate deflation** (Para 4): Focus-grouped smile, vague advancement
5. **Staccato punch** (Final para): Long setup → "It was, after all, not particularly intelligent."

**Vocabulary markers used:**
- "in fact" (2x)
- "quite" (1x)
- "rather" (1x)
- "approximately" (2x)
- "almost certainly" (1x)

**Sentence length distribution:**
- Short (≤10 words): 4 sentences (27%)
- Medium (11-30 words): 7 sentences (47%)
- Long (31-50 words): 3 sentences (20%)
- Very long (>50 words): 1 sentence (6%)
