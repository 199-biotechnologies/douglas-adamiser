# Adams Corpus Analysis Summary

**Source:** 528,808 words from Douglas Adams' works (Hitchhiker's series, Salmon of Doubt, etc.)

## Sentence Length Distribution (CRITICAL)

Adams uses MORE short sentences than commonly assumed:

| Length | Percentage | Target |
|--------|------------|--------|
| Short (≤10 words) | **30.2%** | Aim for 25-35% |
| Medium (11-30 words) | **46.1%** | Aim for 40-50% |
| Long (31-50 words) | **15.3%** | Aim for 12-18% |
| Very long (>50 words) | **8.4%** | Aim for 5-10% |

**Key insight:** Nearly 1/3 of Adams' sentences are short punches. The stereotype of long, winding sentences is partially myth—he balances cumulative builds with rapid-fire short sentences.

## Validation Targets

For authentic Adams style, text should have:

- [ ] 25-35% short sentences (≤10 words)
- [ ] "Quite" or "rather" appearing at least once per 1500 words
- [ ] "In fact" or "of course" appearing at least once per 2000 words
- [ ] 8-10 questions per 1000 words
- [ ] 2-3 em-dashes per 1000 words
- [ ] At least 1 staccato deflation (long sentence → short punch) per 500 words
- [ ] NO MORE than 1-2 "much the same way that X doesn't" per 5000 words

---

## Common Imitation Mistakes (Based on Data)

1. **Overusing "utterly"** - Real Adams uses "quite" and "rather" 10x more often
2. **Overusing brick similes** - Adams uses "much the same way" rarely
3. **Not enough short sentences** - 30% should be punchy
4. **Not enough questions** - Adams asks ~9 per 1000 words
5. **Exotic vocabulary** - Adams uses simple words in absurd contexts
