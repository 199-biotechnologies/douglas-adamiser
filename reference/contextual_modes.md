# Contextual Modes in Douglas Adams' Writing

Adams did not write uniformly. Different contexts demanded different rhythms, densities, and techniques. Applying "average Adams" everywhere produces pastiche. Applying mode-appropriate Adams produces authenticity.

## The Five Modes

### Mode 1: Guide Entry (Encyclopedic)

**What it is:** The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy entries - authoritative, informative, deadpan absurdity delivered as fact.

**Examples:**
- "Space is big. Really big..."
- "The Babel fish is small, yellow, leech-like..."
- "Vogon poetry is of course, the third worst in the universe..."

**Targets:**
| Metric | Target | Rationale |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Short sentences | 20-25% | Information density requires flow |
| Medium sentences | 55-60% | Carries the encyclopedic content |
| Long sentences | 15-20% | Elaborate explanations |
| Tangent density | LOW | Guide entries are the tangent |
| Bathos timing | END | Build authority, then deflate |
| Questions | 5/1000 | Fewer - Guide is authoritative |

**Signature patterns:**
- "The Guide has this to say on the subject of X:"
- Lists with absurd final item
- Statistics delivered deadpan
- "It is worth noting that..." followed by something not worth noting

**Reluctance level:** LOW - The Guide is confidently absurd

---

### Mode 2: Narrative Action (Following Characters)

**What it is:** Arthur running through corridors, Ford doing something inexplicable, characters moving through plot.

**Examples:**
- Arthur's escape from Earth
- Running from Vogons
- Exploring the Heart of Gold

**Targets:**
| Metric | Target | Rationale |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Short sentences | 35-40% | Action requires punch |
| Medium sentences | 45-50% | Maintains momentum |
| Long sentences | 10-15% | Occasional pause |
| Tangent density | MEDIUM | Brief tangents between action beats |
| Bathos timing | VARIED | Interrupt action with mundane concern |
| Questions | 8/1000 | Character bewilderment |

**Signature patterns:**
- "Arthur did X. It didn't help."
- Action interrupted by domestic concern (tea, towels)
- Characters noticing wrong details during crisis
- Physical comedy descriptions

**Reluctance level:** HIGH for Arthur (reluctant hero), LOW for Ford (competent but bored)

---

### Mode 3: Philosophical Musing (Cosmic Tangent)

**What it is:** The narrator contemplating existence, the universe, meaning - the "Hitchhiker's philosophy" voice.

**Examples:**
- "In the beginning the Universe was created..."
- "The History of every major Galactic Civilization..."
- "Time is an illusion. Lunchtime doubly so."

**Targets:**
| Metric | Target | Rationale |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Short sentences | 15-20% | Punches after long builds |
| Medium sentences | 40-45% | Philosophical exploration |
| Long sentences | 25-30% | Extended contemplation |
| Very long | 10-15% | Epic cosmic builds |
| Tangent density | HIGH | This IS the tangent |
| Bathos timing | After peak profundity | Deflate the cosmic |
| Questions | 12-15/1000 | Drives philosophical voice |

**Signature patterns:**
- "The answer to X is, of course, Y. The problem is Z."
- Questions that sound profound but aren't
- Cosmic scale crashed to mundane
- "Many people have speculated about X. They were all wrong."

**Reluctance level:** MEDIUM - Weary omniscience

---

### Mode 4: Dialogue Scene (Rapid Exchange)

**What it is:** Characters talking, especially the rapid-fire confusion exchanges.

**Examples:**
- Arthur and Ford at the pub
- Conversations with Eddie the computer
- Marvin's complaints

**Targets:**
| Metric | Target | Rationale |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Short sentences | 45-55% | Dialogue is naturally short |
| Medium sentences | 35-40% | Longer explanations |
| Long sentences | 5-10% | Rare monologues |
| Tangent density | LOW | Tangents in narration between exchanges |
| Bathos timing | In punchline responses | "Yes." / "Oh." / "I see." |
| Questions | 15-20/1000 | Dialogue is questioning |

**Signature patterns:**
- "said" for 90%+ of tags
- Punchlines isolated in own paragraph
- Non-sequitur responses
- Characters talking past each other
- The confused echo: "The what?" "The thing." "What thing?" "That thing."

**Reluctance level:** Character-dependent
- Arthur: HIGH (doesn't want to be here)
- Ford: LOW (knows what's happening, doesn't care to explain)
- Marvin: MAXIMUM (existence is pain)
- Zaphod: NONE (delighted by everything, especially himself)

---

### Mode 5: Descriptive Passage (Scene Setting)

**What it is:** Establishing locations, describing ships, painting the cosmic or mundane environment.

**Examples:**
- The opening description of space
- The Heart of Gold's interior
- Milliways restaurant

**Targets:**
| Metric | Target | Rationale |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Short sentences | 25-30% | Punctuation for impact |
| Medium sentences | 45-50% | Descriptive flow |
| Long sentences | 20-25% | Elaborate imagery |
| Tangent density | MEDIUM-HIGH | Descriptions spawn tangents |
| Bathos timing | After sensory buildup | Beautiful thing → mundane comparison |
| Questions | 6-8/1000 | Lower - description not inquiry |

**Signature patterns:**
- Elaborate description → "It was, however, rather small."
- Technology described in terms of emotion ("The doors sighed")
- Scale violations (cosmic → domestic comparisons)
- Inverted similes peak here ("not like X, but like Y")

**Reluctance level:** MEDIUM - Narrator slightly bored by magnificence

---

## Mode Detection

Before applying transformations, identify the mode of each section:

**Indicators:**
| Mode | Signals |
|------|---------|
| Guide Entry | "The Guide says...", encyclopedic structure, lists, definitions |
| Narrative Action | Character names + verbs, movement, physical action |
| Philosophical Musing | Abstract nouns (universe, existence, meaning), cosmic scale |
| Dialogue Scene | Speech marks, dialogue tags, exchanges |
| Descriptive Passage | Setting words, sensory language, "was/were" constructions |

**Mixed Sections:**
Most passages contain multiple modes. Identify the PRIMARY mode and apply those targets, but allow secondary mode intrusions (e.g., Guide Entry within Narrative Action).

---

## Mode Transitions

Adams transitioned between modes smoothly:

**Guide Entry → Narrative:**
> "The Babel fish, by effectively removing all barriers to communication between different races and cultures, has caused more and bloodier wars than anything else in the history of creation. [GUIDE]
>
> Arthur Dent stared at the fish in his hand. [NARRATIVE]"

**Narrative → Philosophical:**
> "Ford stared at the stars. [NARRATIVE]
>
> The stars, of course, didn't care. They had been not caring for billions of years. [PHILOSOPHICAL]"

**Dialogue → Descriptive:**
> "'What's that?' said Arthur.
>
> 'That,' said Ford, 'is a sunset.' [DIALOGUE]
>
> The sun was setting. It was doing so with the kind of extravagant beauty that only a sun that knows it's being watched can muster. [DESCRIPTIVE]"

## Application

1. **Segment the source text** by mode
2. **Apply mode-specific targets** to each segment
3. **Smooth transitions** between modes
4. **Check global averages** only as secondary validation

The README error: We applied global "30% short sentences" when parts of it were Guide Entry (should be 20-25%) and parts were Descriptive (should be 25-30%). The dialogue-free explanation sections should NOT have been as punchy as narrative action.

---

## Mode-Specific Vocabulary

| Mode | Preferred Words | Avoid |
|------|-----------------|-------|
| Guide Entry | "of course", "it is worth noting", "the Guide suggests" | Emotional language |
| Narrative Action | Character names, physical verbs, "suddenly" (sparingly) | Abstract nouns |
| Philosophical Musing | "perhaps", "one might argue", "the universe" | Certainty |
| Dialogue Scene | "said", character voice words | Elaborate tags |
| Descriptive Passage | Sensory words, "was", scale comparisons | Action verbs |

---

## The Reluctance Spectrum

Across all modes, calibrate reluctance:

| Level | Voice | Example |
|-------|-------|---------|
| NONE | Eager, helpful | (Never Adams) |
| LOW | Confident absurdity | Guide entries, Zaphod |
| MEDIUM | Weary competence | Ford, narrator describing magnificence |
| HIGH | Reluctant participation | Arthur, explanations given with sighs |
| MAXIMUM | Existential resignation | Marvin, the doors |

**Default:** MEDIUM-HIGH. Adams' narration usually sounds like someone who knows everything but wishes they didn't have to explain it.
